Software Name: Alexandria

**II.         Client Background – History, current state and etc.**

The client for this project is the Department of Computer Information Science and Mathematics (DCISM), which handles various student thesis projects that serve as important academic references for future researchers and students. Currently, access to previous thesis works is mainly done through the library, requiring students to manually search through available documents, which can be time-consuming and inconvenient. To address this issue, the project aims to develop Alexandria, a web-based thesis repository platform designed to provide DCISM students with easier access to previous thesis titles, abstracts, and related information. The system will serve as a centralized digital platform where students can browse and search thesis records without needing to go through the traditional library process. Alexandria will help improve the organization, accessibility, and availability of thesis resources, allowing students to find references, explore research topics, and gain ideas for their own academic projects more efficiently.

III.   	**Statement of Need** – Specific needs for better efficiency, possible solutions to problems, etc. 

Over the years, the client has continuously produced valuable research outputs through student thesis projects. However, trying to access such studies is often inconvenient and can become time-consuming for both students and faculty, which is why the client requires a more efficient way to store and access these projects. To address these issues, there is a need for a centralized digital repository containing thesis studies of past DCISM students. This can help students easily find relevant references they can draw inspiration from and learn from previous researchers’ experiences to make more informed decisions regarding their own research directions. By improving the accessibility of departmental research outputs, the proposed system, “*Alexandria*,” can help preserve institutional knowledge and enhance the overall efficiency of the thesis development process. 

**IV.**  	**The Software as Response to the Need** – State the kind of software that can satisfy the needs of the client.

Alexandria  addresses the challenges of thesis accessibility, topic exploration, and knowledge transfer. It serves as a centralized platform where students can easily access, search, and browse previous thesis projects without relying solely on physical archives or manual inquiries. It also preserves valuable recommendations and lessons learned from previous researchers, allowing future students and thesis takers to benefit from their experiences. By making departmental research more accessible and easier to navigate, the system reduces uncertainty during thesis preparation and supports more informed research decisions.

V.        **The Software’s Main Functionalities** – See Functionalities List Document

Alexandria provides a centralized repository for storing and managing departmental thesis papers and related research information. The system allows authorized users to upload thesis documents together with essential metadata such as titles, authors, abstracts, keywords, and research categories. Students can browse, search, and filter thesis records to quickly identify relevant studies and view detailed information through an integrated PDF preview. Additionally, the platform includes dedicated sections for recommendations and lessons learned. This allows for knowledge transfer between graduating researchers and future thesis taking students while also suggesting related theses based on shared categories and keyword, which separates it from usual repositories and compilation-style libraries.
